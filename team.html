Our Story

Verdict Hub: Where Experience Meets Innovation

Verdict Hub was born at the intersection of deep courtroom expertise and groundbreaking technology and we're not just building technology; we’re scaling the elite, high-touch expertise of one of the world’s leading trial consultants into accessible, actionable intelligence, empowering trial teams everywhere.

Our story begins in 1984 when Robert Hirschhorn, then a young trial lawyer, hired Cathy E. Bennett—one of the pioneers in jury and trial consulting—for what seemed like an unwinnable case. With Cathy's help, Robert secured an improbable victory. Turning to Cathy afterward, he declared, "You've changed my life. I want to do what you do." That moment sparked a partnership that endured until Cathy's passing in 1992.

At that critical crossroad, Robert faced a choice: return to practicing law or carry forward Cathy’s visionary work. He chose to continue her legacy. Over the next four decades, Robert refined the art of jury selection and trial preparation, expertly blending instinct, body language analysis, and deep, practical experience to support trial teams nationwide. Robert often remarked, "I have the greatest job in the world—except for all the travel."

Fast forward to 2024. Swarup and Gaurav, deeply immersed in Generative AI, saw its potential not merely in theoretical models but in solving tangible, real-world problems. They envisioned the courtroom as an ideal frontier for innovation. Around this time, Bloomberg Legal News interviewed Robert on AI's future in jury consulting. Robert boldly predicted AI would replace consultants overly reliant on rigid juror profiles, while emphasizing that genuine jury consulting is fundamentally about understanding people, nuance, and the human factor.

When Swarup and Gaurav came across Robert’s insights, they immediately recognized an ideal partner—someone whose vision and vast experience perfectly matched their ambition to build transformative AI solutions. Upon meeting, Robert remained skeptical about whether Generative AI had matured enough to accurately model juror behavior across various jurisdictions. To test this, he provided Swarup and Gaurav anonymized data from past online surveys, outcomes of which had already been validated by focus groups or trial verdicts.

The results were astonishing. Without access to any information beyond what survey participants received—and the venue—Swarup and Gaurav's system provided insights that came remarkably close to the actual results in terms of apportionment and damages.

That breakthrough led to the creation of Verdict Hub—a fusion of Robert’s extensive jury consulting expertise with Swarup and Gaurav’s vision and now their patent-pending AI technology. Verdict Hub represents a new era in jury and trial consulting, blending human wisdom with technological precision.

Our Team

Robert Hirschhorn

Jury and Trial Consultation Expert

Robert Hirschhorn of Cathy E. Bennett & Associates, Inc. is an attorney and nationally recognized expert in jury and trial consultation. Since 1985, he has helped select juries that have returned tens of billions of dollars in verdicts across the country. His recent work includes extraordinary results such as a $2.065 billion RoundUp verdict in Georgia (2025), a $109.5 million verdict for a client severely burned in a house explosion (2025), and a $60 million award in the first Enfamil baby formula case (2024).

Other notable outcomes include a $2.25 billion and a $175 million RoundUp verdict in Philadelphia (2024, 2023), an $860 million crane collapse case in Dallas (2023), a $440 million wrongful death verdict for a child (2023), and a $110 million award against 3M for two soldiers injured by defective military earplugs (2023). Earlier landmark verdicts include a $352 million award in Cruz v. Allied for a catastrophically injured man (2021), a $525 million patent infringement verdict in the Eastern District of Texas (2021), a $222 million wrongful death verdict (2021), and a $115 million award against the State of Washington (2020).

Robert was also part of the trial team that secured an $8 billion punitive damages verdict against Johnson & Johnson in a Risperdal case (2019), selected the jury in the first opioid litigation trial that settled for $260 million (2019), and helped achieve $550 million in actual and $4.6 billion in punitive damages for 22 plaintiffs in the Johnson & Johnson talc powder litigation (2018).

In the criminal arena, Robert has consulted in several nationally watched trials resulting in acquittals, including State of Florida v. William Kennedy Smith (sexual assault), State of Texas v. U.S. Senator Kay Bailey Hutchison (ethics charges), State of Texas v. Robert Durst (murder), United States v. Robert Kaluza (Deepwater Horizon explosion), and United States v. Mark Forkner (Boeing 737 Max). A frequent speaker and thought leader, Robert has authored four books and numerous articles for Law360, Texas Lawyer, and AAJ.

He has been featured on Good Morning America, MSNBC, Court TV, CNN, HLN, Dateline NBC, 48 Hours, Nightline, and national radio. He lectures across the country on the art and science of jury selection.

Gaurav Tiwari

Product and Technology Executive

Gaurav Tiwari is a product and technology executive turned entrepreneur. In his last venture, he founded an E-commerce platform (The Waves) that merges inspiring content with proprietary AI technology to make discovering and shopping organically-farmed, honest wines from the world's most groundbreaking winemakers easier, and more personalized, than ever before.

Prior to founding The Waves Gaurav spent nearly a decade at the E-commerce tech giant Amazon, where he led teams responsible for category management, technology, data science, and product development. Before Amazon, Gaurav was a management consultant at Strategy& (formerly known as Booz & Company). Prior to that, he worked at Mars Inc., where he led operations management for the M&M's brand.

Gaurav holds a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from the Institute of Engineering and Technology, India, a Dual Masters in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research from The Pennsylvania State University and MBA from The Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. Gaurav lives in Seattle with his family — three energetic kids and a sweet little pup.
